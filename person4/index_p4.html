Mohandas Karamchand Gandhi (/ˈɡɑːndi, ˈɡændi/;[2] 2 October 1869 – 30 January 1948) was an Indian lawyer,[3] anti-colonial nationalist,[4] and political ethicist,[5] who employed nonviolent resistance to lead the successful campaign for India's independence from British rule,[6] and in turn inspired movements for civil rights and freedom across the world. The honorific Mahātmā (Sanskrit: "great-souled", "venerable"), first applied to him in 1914 in South Africa, is now used throughout the world

3 things that inspire me about Ghandi

  1. His multifarious and dynamic personality was based on truth and nothing but the truth
  2. Non – violence was another intrinsic element of this philosophy.
  3. The simplicity of his life, always following his own teaching, summarized by "My Life is my Message"

Ghandi's 3 main accomplishments

  • Non-cooperation movement
  • Salt march to Dandi
  • Fight again untouchability
